We Are In A Mission To Help Helpless

Established in July 2013, the Association for Development and Health Intervention (ASDHI) is a community-led organization committed to transforming lives and communities through innovative and inclusive initiatives. Convinced that youth are the driving force for a better future and women are the main pillars of society, ASDHI supports children, women, and youth in urban and rural areas who often confront challenges. The organization equips them with the skills, tools, and opportunities to meet today's challenges and build a better future. ASDHI's key objectives are to: Promote Equality and Access by advocating for improved resource access and equal opportunities for marginalized groups, while protecting the environment and promoting sustainable development; Enhance Health and Wellness through implementing comprehensive health and mental health programs to support community well-being, leveraging technology to strengthen the delivery and impact of these initiatives; and Combat Gender-Based Violence by preventing, responding to, and advocating against gender-based violence in the community. Through these holistic and inclusive approaches, ASDHI aims to empower children, women, and youth to thrive and lead the way towards a more equitable and sustainable future.

The organization has made a significant impact on several Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). For education (SDG 4), it has supported over 5,000 children with school supplies, advocated for the reintegration of pregnant girls, and provided free education to 1,800 disadvantaged youth. Regarding gender equality (SDG 5), it raises awareness among 1,000 people annually, combats sexual violence, and provides support to victims through a mental health platform. In health and well-being (SDG 3), the organization facilitated HIV screenings for 200,000 people and distributed over a million condoms. In employment (SDG 8), it has trained and employed 750 youth, supported 8,000 entrepreneurs, and assisted 20,000 displaced individuals. Finally, in climate action (SDG 13), the organization conducts ecological initiatives and trains young people in COP negotiations. These efforts reflect a comprehensive approach to tackling key societal issues, fostering sustainable change and development. Through these actions, the organization contributes to improving lives and building stronger communities. The organization's work also addresses both immediate needs and long-term challenges, ensuring lasting positive impact. Its focus on empowerment, education, and well-being provides a holistic solution to pressing global issues. This commitment plays a vital role in driving progress toward the achievement of the SDGs
